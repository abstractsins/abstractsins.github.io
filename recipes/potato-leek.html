Potato Leek

  • Prep: 10 minutes
  • Cook: 1 hour
  • Calories: 209
  • Protein: 5g
  • Carbs: 26g
  • Fat: 9g
  • Fiber: 2.6
  • * per serving
  • Makes: 8 servings
  • Each Serving: about 12oz

Ingredients/Quantities

  • mushrooms: 2 cups
  • leeks: 16 oz, trimmed
  • yellow potatoes: 20 oz
  • vegetable stock: 1 quart
  • butter: 4 tablespoons
  • buttermilk: 1 cup
  • half & half: 1 cup
  • kosher salt:
  • snipped chives: for garnish

This is my winter staple. It's incredibly simple and hearty and easy to make a lot of.

Potato skins are very nutritious, you can leave them on in a chunkier version (see the variation below).

Procedure

  • Trim the leeks and discard the hard, dark green parts. Chop the leeks.
  • Peel and cut the potatoes into medium chunks.
  • Cook the leeks in butter on medium-high until soft, and then on medium until very soft, about 15 minutes.
  • Add the vegetable stock and potatoes and increase heat to bring to a boil.
  • SECRET: Making your own vegetable stock is easy and rewarding.
  • Reduce heat, cover, and simmer until potatoes are fully cooked - about 45 minutes.
  • Take off heat and puree until smooth. Stir in buttermilk and cream.
  • Serve hot, with snipped chives.

Hardware

  • large pot
  • stirring spoon
  • immersion blender
  • measuring cups

You may also need...

  • strainer
  • potato masher

Variations

  • Cut up a pound of bacon into small pieces and cook them first. Cook the leeks in the bacon fat and add the bacon back to the finished soup.
  • Saute exotic mushrooms with the leeks and puree along with everything else.
  • Leave the potato skins on and don't puree. Mash by hand and leave chunky.
