My Meatloaf

  • Prep: 15 minutes
  • Cook: 40 minutes
  • Calories: 423
  • Protein: 44g
  • Carbs: 15g
  • Fat: 20g
  • Fiber: 1.3g
  • * per serving
  • Makes: 6 servings
  • Each Serving: about 5oz

Ingredients/Quantities

  • ground beef (lean): 2 pounds
  • breadcrumbs: 1/2 cup
  • large egg: 1, scrambled
  • yellow onion: 1 cup, small dice
  • milk: 1 cup
  • frozen peas: 1/2 cup
  • Worcestershire Sauce: 1 tablespoon
  • ketchup: 1/4 cup
  • malt vinegar: 1/4 cup
  • garlic powder: 1 teaspoon
  • mustard powder: 1/2 tablespoon
  • ground coriander: 1/2 teaspoon
  • ground cumin: 1/2 teaspoon
  • ground sage: 1/4 teaspoon
  • black pepper: 1 teaspoon
  • kosher salt: 1 teaspoon

This meatloaf is so simple and delicious that it's my top dinner fallback, with plenty of possibilities for variation. The glaze adds a tangy punch, while the breadcrumbs hold in the moisture.

If you like a nice crust, make sure to bake uncovered with high heat.

Procedure

  • Start by heating oven to 375° F. Then measure all the spices together, except for the paprika.
  • Dice up the onion and saute it in a little oil over medium-high heat, with a pinch of salt and pepper. Once the onions are soft and slightly browned, add the paprika and remove from heat. Put to the side to cool.
  • Mix together the brown sugar, malt vinegar, and ketchup in a bowl to make a glaze. Set aside.
  • In a large bowl, mix together all other ingredients, along with the fried onions. Once mixture is uniform, place in a loaf pan. Pour glaze over top.
  • SECRET: Lining the pan with two layers of parchment paper makes turning out the loaf a breeze. Easier clean-up too.
  • Bake in the oven, uncovered, for 30-40 minutes, or until a meat thermometer reads 155° F.
  • Let rest for 10 minutes until turning out the loaf and slicing.

Variations

  • Instead of the glaze, top with mashed potatoes and bake like Shepard's Pie.
  • Replace the spice blend with canned adobo sauce.
  • Can also be made without breadcrumbs. Add a little extra egg and mix thoroughly for a smooth texture.
