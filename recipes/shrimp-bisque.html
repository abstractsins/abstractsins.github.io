Shrimp Bisque

  • Prep: 20 minutes
  • Cook: 40 minutes
  • Calories: 367
  • Protein: 28g
  • Carbs: 15g
  • Fat: 20g
  • Fiber: 2g
  • * per serving
  • Makes: 6 servings
  • Each Serving: about 10oz

Ingredients/Quantities

  • shrimp: 1-1/2 pounds
  • leeks: 2 cups, trimmed and chopped
  • garlic: 3 cloves, chopped
  • mushrooms: 2 cups
  • paprika: 1/2 tablespoon
  • cayenne: 1/4 teaspoon
  • white wine: 1/4 cup
  • Grand Marinier: 1/4 cup
  • shrimp stock: 4 cups
  • fresh rosemary: 2 teaspoons
  • fresh sage: 1 tablespoon
  • tomato paste: 2 tablespoons
  • butter: 3 tablespoons
  • flour: 3 tablespoons
  • olive oil: 2 tablespoons
  • kosher salt: 1 tablespoon
  • black pepper: 1/2 teaspoon

This is a luxurious shrimp bisque that I always look forward to making. You can use smaller shrimp since you will be blending them, but gor for the best quality you can find.

Shrimp stock itself can be difficult to find. Make it yourself if you have access to good quality whole shrimp. Or fortify a seafood stock with shrimp shells.

Procedure

  • Cook leeks and mushrooms down in olive oil, season with paprika and salt.
  • Add garlic, cook until fragrant.
  • Add shrimp and cayenne.
  • When shrimp is cooked, add wine and liquor. Reduce by half.
  • SECRET: For a chunkier texture, reserve half od the shrimp and vegetables at this time.
  • Add stock and tomato paste, bring to a simmer and add herbs.
  • Blend with an immersion blender to desired consistency. Add reserverd ingredients and blend again briefly.
  • In a small skillet, melt the butter and add the flour. Stir until sand-like and then cook on medium heat until blonde. Stir into soup and bring to a boil for the roux to thicken the soup.

Hardware

  • large pot
  • immersion blender
  • stirring spoon
  • small skillet

You may also need...

  • measuring cups
  • measuring spoons
  • paring knife
  • can opener

Variations

  • Add chunked potatoes and simmer for a more chowder-like soup.
  • Instead of half milk, use all half & half.
  • Replace the leeks with onion and fennel, and replace the rosemary with fennel herb.
