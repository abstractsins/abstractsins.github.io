Best Biscuits

  • Prep: 30 minutes
  • Cook: 20 minutes
  • Calories: 205
  • Protein: 6g
  • Carbs: 22g
  • Fat: 10g
  • Fiber: 1g
  • * per serving
  • Makes: 8 servings
  • Each Serving: about 2 oz

Ingredients/Quantities

  • bread flour: 4 oz
  • pastry flour: 4 oz
  • sugar: 1/2 oz
  • baking powder: 1/2 oz
  • kosher salt: 1 teaspoon
  • black pepper: 1/2 tablespoon, coarsely ground
  • smoked cheese: 2-1/4 oz, grated
  • butter: 3 oz, cold
  • milk: 5 oz, ice cold

You can easily find blocks of smoked cheddar, mozzarella, or gouda at just about any supermarket these days. I use plenty of black pepper, but feel free to adjust it to your taste.

Cold milk and butter make flakier biscuits. Warm ingredients blend together and make a mealy dough.

Procedure

  • The convenient part of this recipe is that the dry ingredients can all be measured out ahead of time. Simply combine them all in a mason jar until you're ready to use the mix. You can also cut the butter into small pieces the night before and store in the fridge.
  • Preheat the oven to 375° F. Take the dry ingredients and sift together.
  • Mix or cut small pieces of cold butter into the dry mix until it is grainy like sand.
  • Add milk and mix just enough to form a dough. If using a machine, stop it and mix by hand until the dough can be turned out on to the counter.
  • SECRET: Resting the dough in the fridge between mixing stages will help keep the final product flaky.
  • Once the dough is just solid enough, roll out it out into a square. A nice touch is to make multiple folds and rolling it out between each layer, like a croissant. This laminating process makes for increased flakiness. It's up to you, just make sure the dough doesn't get too warm.
  • Cut out dough pieces with a cookie-cutter or cut edges off with a pastry wheel so the biscuits rise higher. Chill and re-use, or freeze, any trimmed dough. Or freeze portions to bake on demand.
  • Chill cut-outs again before baking 15-20 minutes at 350°-375° F. Turn baking tray halfway through. Biscuits are done when the tops are a toasty brown.

Variations

  • Omit the pepper and cheese for the best plain biscuit recipe.
  • Buttermilk can be substituted for a fluffier biscuit.
  • Replace up to half of the bread flour with whole wheat flour.
