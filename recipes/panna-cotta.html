Panna Cotta {with Caramel}

  • Prep: 15 minutes
  • Cook: 4 hours - overnight
  • Calories: 367
  • Protein: 3g
  • Carbs: 13g
  • Fat: 31g
  • Fiber: 0g
  • * per serving
  • Makes: 6 servings
  • Each Serving: about 4 oz

Ingredients/Quantities

  • heavy cream: 2 cups
  • half & half: 1 cup
  • sugar: 1/3 cup
  • vanilla extract: 1-1/2 teaspoons
  • water: 2 tablespoons, cold
  • gelatin powder: 1 tablespoon
    • for the caramel:
  • sugar: 4 oz
  • water: 1 oz
  • lemon juice: 1/4 teaspoon
  • milk: 2 oz

This is such an easy dish to make that it's a natural fallback. But it must be planned ahead because the gelatin needs time to set. Because of that though, it's easy to make a lot ahead of an occasion and have them ready.

This can be a very rich dessert, with the cream. But it can be skimmed back with lowfat milk and buttermilk. The touch of caramel or fruit sauce gives it a bit of a spark.

Procedure

  • In a small bowl, sprinkle the gelatin over the water and let bloom for 5 minutes.
  • Heat the cream, half & half and the sugar just to a boil over medium heat while stirring. Remove from the heat and stir in the bloomed gelatin and the vanilla.
  • After making sure everything is mixed and dissolved, pour into 6 individual serving dishes and then chill, covered, at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.
  • For the caramel, bring the sugar, water, and lemon juice to a boil in a heavy sauce pan, stirring continuously. Cook to a golden color but do not burn.
  • SECRET: Caramel is just sugar: it is energy, pure and cheap. If it starts to burn, it will quickly burn and easily turn to black carbon. Even if it's just a little burnt, it will have a noticibly off flavor. Don't waste the cream on caramel that may be a little too dark. Just take a few minutes to start over.
  • Turn off heat and add half of the cream. It will sputter and boil but keep stirring. Then add the remaining cream, stirring. Return to low heat if necessary.
  • Add a pinch of salt then let cool completely. Goes great in a squeeze bottle.
  • When serving, let panna cotta and caramel come up to room temperature.

Variations

  • Because this dish is so simple, there are a lot of options for varation. Instead of a caramel sauce, make a fruit sauce, like raspberry.
  • The plain cream is delicious enough but can be flavored with things like orange or lemon zest, coffee, or liqueurs.
  • Prepare a graham cracker, oreo, or nilla wafer crust in the buttom of the ramekin before pouring the panna cotta and enjoy a mini pie.
