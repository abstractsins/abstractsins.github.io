Langos

  • Prep: 3 hours
  • Cook: 20 minutes
  • Calories: 226
  • Protein: 8g
  • Carbs: 46g
  • Fat: 0g
  • Fiber: 2g
  • * per serving
  • Makes: 4 servings
  • Each Serving: about 3 oz

Ingredients/Quantities

  • Water: 1/2 cup, 80°-90° F
  • flour: 2 cups
  • sugar: 1/2 tablespoon
  • kosher salt: 1teaspoon
  • dry yeast: 1 packet

As plain as this Hungarian flatbread is, it is cherished by my family and regarded as a long-awaited celebration whenever something important enough arises.

In Hungary, this has become a street food, available with an array of toppings: from melted cheese to peanut butter. Our family traditionally just serves it warm, with salt and sliced onion.

Procedure

  • Mix the warm water, yeast, and sugar together. Let sit 15 minutes.
  • If using a mixer, combine everything into mixing bowl and mix on low until everything is combined. Increase speed to medium and mix for about 8 minutes.
  • If mixing by hand, add salt and flour and work it in with more warm water 1 Tbsp at a time until you have a smooth workable dough that you can form into a round ball. Don't use too much water.
  • Cover the dough and place in a warm place for about 2 hours or until twice the size.
  • SECRET: Instead of waiting for the dough to rise, you can perform a slow rise in the fridge from the night before.
  • Turn out the dough and punch it down. Portion out into 4-8 equal pieces. Let the pieces rest for 30 minutes.
  • Roll the pieces out into circles 1/4" thick, to be pan-fried. Let them rest another half hour.
  • Heat vegetable oil in a skillet and then fry each dough round on medium to medium-high one at a time, flipping it over until golden-brown on both side and cooked through. As bubble puff up in the dough, poke them with a fork to deflate them. The resulting texture is part of what makes langos so good.
  • Place finished langos on paper towels to absorb excess oil and cool. Enjoy warm.

Variations

  • One common variation is potato langos, where boiled potatoes are mashed into the dough.
  • Substitute up to 50% of the flour with whole wheat.
  • Instead of water, try with milk.
