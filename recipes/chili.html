Kwiki-Mart Chili

  • Prep: 15 minutes
  • Cook: 3 hours
  • Calories: 550
  • Protein: 45g
  • Carbs: 5g
  • Fat: 26g
  • Fiber: 13g
  • * per serving
  • Makes: 12 servings
  • Each Serving: about 8oz

Ingredients/Quantities

  • ground beef (80/20): 3 pounds
  • beef stock: 3 quarts
  • yellow onion: 1-1/2 cup, medium dice
  • garlic: 4 clove cup, minced
  • pinto beans: 14 oz cup
  • red beans: 14 oz cup
  • black beans: 14 oz tablespoon
  • chipotles in adobo: 1 can cup
  • mustard powder: 1-1/2 teaspoon
  • smoked paprika: 1-1/2 teaspoon
  • ground coriander: 3/4 teaspoon
  • ground cumin: 3/4 teaspoon
  • cocoa powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • black pepper: 1-1/2 teaspoon
  • malt vinegar: 1 tablespoon
  • kosher salt: 1 tablespoon

I first made this chili for my friend's birthday in a cabin deep in the mountains, with ingredients from the nearest gas station market.

The true character of this dish comes from paitiently allowing the stock the cook down, thickening the chili without starch or anything extra.

Procedure

  • Start by measuring all your spices together and cutting up the vegetables.
  • Cook the beef in a dutch oven on medium-high heat to release the fat and cook the beef with some salt and pepper.
  • Add the onion and cook them in the fat with beef. Maintain enough heat to cook down the onions but not burn anything. Make sure to break up the meat for a uniform texture. Add the garlic and cook briefly, just until fragrant.
  • If using canned beans, drain them, and then add to the pot along with all the spices. Add enough stock to cover everything, then turn up the heat to bring to a boil.
  • SECRET: Good stock should be as low in salt as possible. As it cooks down, any saltiness will become concentrated. Cook with salt, and adjust to taste.
  • Once boiling, bring down to a medium-low simmer. The full simmering process will take 2 to 3 hours, during which you will skim off the fat and junk that accumulates on the surace as it cooks.
  • As the stock cooks down you will need to add more until you've used all the stock and it has reduced to a nice stew-like thickness, or to your liking.

Variations

  • White beans are also an excellent choice.
  • Make spicier with diced fresh chiles or whole dry chiles.
  • Try substituting other cuts of beef, like smoked brisket.
