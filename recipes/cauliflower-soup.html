Moroccan Cauliflower

  • Prep: 15 minutes
  • Cook: 30 minutes
  • Calories: 682
  • Protein: 29g
  • Carbs: 50g
  • Fat: 44g
  • Fiber: 9g
  • * per serving
  • Makes: 4 servings
  • Each Serving: about 12 oz

Ingredients/Quantities

  • cauliflower: 3 heads
  • yellow onion: 1 small, rough chop
  • garlic: 4 cloves
  • half & half: 1 quart
  • milk: 1-1/2 quarts
  • olive oil: 4 tablespoons
  • mustard powder: 1 teaspoon
  • ground coriander: 1 teaspoon
  • ground cumin: 1 teaspoon
  • paprika: 1 tablespoon
  • cinnamon: 1/4 teaspoon
  • ground ginger: 1/2 teaspoon
  • black pepper: 1 teaspoon
  • kosher salt: 1 tablespoon
  • cilantro: for garnish
  • pine nuts: for garnish

I came up with this recipe in culinary school while studying Mediterranean cuisine. The spice blend can be made up ahead of time and used all year! Aside from that it's a very simple recipe.

The spice blend in this recipe is an example of "ras el hanout" - the Arabic term for "top shelf" spices. The exact blend is up to the individual.

Procedure

  • Break the cauliflower into florets and toss with the oil, onion, garlic cloves, and spices. Bake at 350Â° F until soft. Avoid too much color.
  • Bring milk and cream to a boil in a pot on the stove and then reduce to a simmer.
  • Add the roasted vegetables to the pot. Continue simmering for about 10-15 minutes, or until cauliflower is completely cooked through.
  • Take off the heat and then puree.
  • SECRET: Use an immersion blender to get it done quicker and cleaner.
  • Toast the pine nuts in a small dry skillet. Roll up the cilantro leaved and slice into thin wrinkles.
  • Season with salt to taste. Serve hot, garnished with cilantro and pine nuts.

Hardware

  • large pot
  • stirring spoon
  • immersion blender
  • baking sheet
  • small skillet

You may also need...

Variations

  • Omit the spices for a milder cream of cauliflower soup.
  • Adjust the balance of milk and cream or greek yogurt.
  • Infuse herbs like cilantro and mint into oil for a delicate garnish.
