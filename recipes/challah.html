Challah

  • Prep: 5 hours
  • Cook: 25-30 minutes
  • Calories: 262
  • Protein: 8g
  • Carbs: 39g
  • Fat: 7g
  • Fiber: 2g
  • * per serving
  • Makes: 12 servings
  • Each Serving: about 3 oz

Ingredients/Quantities

  • bread flour: 20 oz
  • egg yolk: 4 oz
  • sugar: 1-3/4 oz
  • kosher salt: 1/2 oz
  • water: 8 oz, 80°-90° F
  • vegetable oil: 2 oz
  • dry yeast: 1 packet

This challah is a rich yellow color from the egg yolks. For a less rich dough you can create a balance with yolks and egg whites. This will make a stronger dough.

This is a fun time to practice your braiding skills. Try it with 3, 4, and 6 strands!

Procedure

  • Following the straight-dough method, combine all the ingredients together in the mixing bowl and start mixing on low.
  • After a minute or two, when all the ingredients are combined, increase speed to medium and mix for about another 8 minutes. The dough will be strong from the egg and high-protein flour. If it becomes too much of a struggle for the mixer, continue by hand.
  • Leave the dough in the mixing bowl and cover with plastic or a towl. Please in a warm spot for it to rise 2-3 hours, folding the dough and turning it halfway through.
  • Turn out the dough and portion it into an equal weight for each braid strand. Roll them out to equal lengths and let rest 15 minutes or more until they are easy to work with.
  • SECRET: Straight, equally long strands, tapered at the ends, are crucial to a good braid.
  • After resting, braid the challah to your liking and then cover with plastic or a towel. Leave in a warm, humid place for another hour to proof.
  • Preheat oven and then before baking, brush the dough with egg wash, filling in all the cracks.
  • Bake times will vary depending on size. Internal temperature should reach 190° F. Tapping on the bottom should sound hollow. If outside is browning too quickly, cover with foil. Rotate tray halfway through.

Hardware

  • baking sheet
  • rolling pin

Variations

  • Before placing the dough in the oven, sprinkle poppy or sesame seeds on top of the egg wash.
  • High Holidays: use honey for sugar. Roll the dough flat, sprinkle with raisins and roll up like a cinnamon roll. Then Roll it into a spiral (pictured).
  • Portion into small rolls instead of a large loaf.
