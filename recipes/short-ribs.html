Red Wine Short Ribs

  • Prep: 15 minutes
  • Cook: 2-3 hours
  • Calories: 400
  • Protein: 35g
  • Carbs: 1g
  • Fat: 13g
  • Fiber: 2g
  • * per serving
  • Makes: 6 servings
  • Each Serving: 1 rib

Ingredients/Quantities

  • bone-in beef short ribs: 6 pieces
  • celery: 1 cup, chopped
  • yellow onion: 2 cups, chopped
  • garlic: 4 cloves, chopped
  • carrots: 1 cup, chopped
  • beef stock: 1 quart
  • mushrooms: 1 cup, chopped
  • red wine: 1-1/2 cup
  • fennel seeds: 1 teaspoon
  • fresh rosemary: 1 tablespoon, chopped
  • coriander seeds: 1 teaspoon
  • cumin seeds: 1/2 teaspoon
  • fresh sage: 2 tablespoons
  • black peppercorns: 1 teaspoon
  • black mustard seeds: 1 teaspoon
  • tomato paste: 2 tablespoons
  • kosher salt: 1 tablespoon
  • flour: 4 tablespoons
  • butter: 4 tablespoons

This is the kind of fancy restaurant dish that goes for a pretty penny. But it's easy to make at home - impress yourself, and a date!

Short rib is a delicious, but tough, cut of beef. The gentle simmering process of braising makes the meat more tender.

Turning the braising liquid into a sauce is a classic technique, and very rewarding. However if you don't want to take the time with it, the meat will be ready to eat with your favorite sauce after braising.

Procedure

  • Roughly chop the vegetables into medium-small pieces. Put aside a little bit of each vegetable for making a sauce later. Vegetables will be strained out so a rough chop works fine.
  • The first part of braising is to sear the meat. This can be done on the stove but is much easier and cleaner with a broiler. While the broiler is preheating, season all sides of the ribs with salt.
  • Using the broiler, sear the ribs as fully as possible on all sides. This may take 10-15 minutes on each side, depending on the cuts of meat and your broiler. Remember that you are not trying to cook the ribs through at this point. You are just trying to get a deep sear all the way around. Even if the meat is fully cooked, it is very tough at this point. This part can take 20 minutes. Because of the high heat, things may get a little smoky.
  • While the ribs are searing, heat about 1/4 cup of oil in the dutch oven. Add the mustard seeds to the oil.
  • The mustard seeds will start to pop in the hot oil. Now add the celery and carrot with a pinch of salt and cook until they begin to soft.
  • Add the mushrooms and cook until they begin to soften.
  • Add the onion with a pinch of salt and cook until they are soft. Add garlic and cook until fragrant.
  • Mix in the tomato paste and stir briefly to cook the paste. Add the coriander, cumin, and fennel seeds. Stir briefly until fragrant.
  • Add a little of the wine and stir as it bubbles, then add the rest. Bring to a boil, lower to simmer, and reduce liquid by half. Add the beef stock.
  • If you have your ribs out of the oven by now, add them to the pot. They do not need to be compltely covered in the braising liquid. Add the herbs. Bring to a boil.
  • Lower the oven to 350Â° F. Put foil over the dutch over and put the lid on top. Put the whole pot in the oven. The oven temperature will keep the liquid at a simmer, and the tight lid seal will keep the liquid from reducing. Check tenderness around 90 minutes.
  • Once the ribs have finished braising, remove them from the pot. Skim the fat off the top and strain the braising liquid as finely as you can. Use the vegetables yo usaved from earlier and saute them in a little oil. Deglaze with some wine and then add the braising liquid. Turn up heat and reduce until desired consistency. The butter and flour may be used to make a thickening roux.
  • SECRET: Instead of skimming the fat, you can put it in the fridge and the next day you can pull away the hardened fat.

Variations

  • Instead of red wine, use a strong dark beer, like a Baltic Porter. Also use malt vinegar in place of red wine vinegar.
  • Less fatty boneless short ribs also work. More meat, but less delicious.
  • Finish your refined sauce with currant jelly.
