CCPB Cookies

  • Prep: 20 minutes
  • Cook: 15 minutes
  • Calories: 448
  • Protein: 7g
  • Carbs: 53g
  • Fat: 25g
  • Fiber: 3g
  • * per serving
  • Makes: 12 servings
  • Each Serving: about 2 cookies

Ingredients/Quantities

  • butter: 8 oz
  • white sugar: 3/4 cup
  • brown sugar: 3/4 cup
  • kosher salt: 1/2 teaspoon
  • eggs: 2
  • vanilla extract: 1 teaspoon
  • all-purpose flour: 1 cup
  • whole wheat flour: 1 cup
  • baking soda: 1/2 teaspoon
  • chocolate chips: 1 cup
  • peanut butter chips: 1 cup

I love these cookies because they're so simple, and I think the whole wheat makes them taste even better. The equal part whole wheat flour makes this more nutrituous without sacrificing texture.

You can roll up the dough into a log and cut coins or you can scoop with a spoon and drop on a baking sheet. Parchment paper rules.

Procedure

  • Preheat the oven from 350° F for bigger cookies to 400° F for smaller cookies. Make sure butter is at room temperature, but do not melt.
  • Cream the butter with the sugar and salt on low speed with a paddle, or by hand, until smooth.
  • Add the beaten eggs and incorporate. Mixture will be wet and soupy.
  • The dry ingredients don't have to be sifted, but you should cut the baking soda in with the flours. Add the dry ingredients to the mixer a little at a time to avoid making a mess. But don't overmix or cookies will become tough.
  • SECRET: Whole wheat actually helps prevent the dough from becoming tough too quickly because the bran slows the formation of gluten.
  • Add the chips and then mix just long enough to incorporate evenly.
  • Spoon batter into balls and then chill before baking. Warm batter will spread more and rise less. Bake until nearly dark brown. The whole wheat and the brown sugar make these look done sooner than they really are. A dozen cookies should be done in about 10-15 minutes at 375° F. Turn the sheet halfway through.

Variations

  • Instead of chocolate chips or peanut butter chips, add M&M's.
  • If you like white chocolate, this is a good recipe for substituting white chocolate chips.
  • If you are a fan of nuts, I recommend trying cashews and macadamia nuts.
