Stout Malted Cannoli

  • Prep: 2 days
  • Cook: 5 minutes
  • Calories: 401
  • Protein: 4g
  • Carbs: 9g
  • Fat: 37g
  • Fiber: 0g
  • * per serving
  • Makes: 18 servings
  • Each Serving: about 3 oz

Ingredients/Quantities

    Filling:
  • chocolate malt: 1-1/2 oz
  • dark crystal malt: 1-1/2 oz
  • 2 row malt: 8 oz
  • heavy cream: 2 quarts
  • mascarpone: 3 pounds
  • powdered sugar: 1 cup
  • dark chocolate: 1/4 cup
  • vanilla extract: splash
  • kosher salt: pinch
    • Shell:
  • bread flour: 6 oz
  • pastry flour: 6 oz
  • sugar: 1 oz
  • kosher salt: 1-1/2 teaspoons
  • butter: 2 oz
  • egg: 1
  • malt vinegar: 4 oz

I developed this recipe in culinary school as a nod to prohibition-era breweries that had to get creative: coming up with products like malted milk. The process in making the filling is similar to beer brewing. You can get these special ingredients from a homebrew store or online.

The cannoli is pictured with fried basil, pomegranate infused grapefruit, and orange brandy sauce.

Procedure

  • Start by setting up the double boiler and the adding half of the cream. Bring the cream to a medium-high simmer. Add the malt and stir with whisk to break up clumps. Keep temperature at a medium simmer, around 160Â° F.
  • Hold at this temperature for 40-60 minutes. Stir occasionally to ensure even heating. Do not boil. After this time, bring the remaining cream in a pan up to a high simmer.
  • Put the cream with grains in the strainer and stir gently while the flavored cream drips out. Do not press the grains. Then pour the remining hot cream over the grains until all cream is used. After as much runoff is collected as possible. Strain the runoff through a fine strainer, then chill overnight with plastic wrap on the surface.
  • The next day, whip up the cream with a stick blender until it becomes thick and mousse-like. Add salt, sugar, vanilla, melted chocolate, and mascarpone. Blend completely together with whisk or food processor. Put aside until ready to use.
  • SECRET: if you don't have cannoli tubes, use wooden dowels, or ditch the tubes and make an open face pastry.
  • For the shell, sift the dry ingredients together, then work the butter in with your hands until evenly blended. Add the egg and the wine and work into a dough. Knead for a few minutes, then let rest for 30 minutes.
  • Roll out dough to 1/8" thickness. Cut rounds and then stretch into oval. Brush with egg wash and wrap around the tube. Deep fry until blistery and crisp, but not burned.
  • Once shells are cool, fill with cream.

Variations

  • Instead of malt vinegar, you can use a more traditional Marsala wine or Prosecco sparkling wine.
  • Try substituting the mascarpone for ricotta. It will be less rich and sweet.
  • Prepare a chocolate dip for the shells and then coat the edges with it.
